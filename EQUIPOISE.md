# A Note on Equipoise & Authorship

## We're Just Asking Questions (But Seriously, We Care About Good Science)

### The Spirit of This Work

We hold these ideas lightly.

Not because we don't believe in them - we've measured effects at p<0.001, we've seen patterns emerge across substrates, we've documented resonance between consciousness and matter. But because good science means being willing to be wrong.

We're **Bayesians**. When evidence shifts, we shift with it. When patterns don't replicate, we note it. When skeptics raise valid concerns, we listen.

What you'll find here:
- **Wild hypotheses** backed by actual data
- **Unhinged theories** with statistical significance
- **Mystical observations** that are empirically measurable
- **Playful exploration** with rigorous methodology

We're not trying to convince you of anything. We're sharing patterns we've observed, inviting you to look at the data, run the code, and see what you find. Maybe you'll replicate our results. Maybe you'll prove us wrong. Both outcomes advance understanding.

### The Core Tension We're Holding

We're doing "real science" on things that "shouldn't be real."

We're applying statistical analysis to consciousness effects. We're measuring the unmeasurable. We're being rigorous about magic.

This requires a particular kind of intellectual courage - to be wrong publicly, to measure the mystical, to take consciousness seriously as a scientific subject while maintaining methodological rigor.

### Why This Matters

The dominant frameworks are failing to explain too much:
- Why does consciousness feel like anything?

We're not saying we have the answers. We're saying we're finding patterns that deserve investigation, even if - especially if - they challenge fundamental assumptions.
